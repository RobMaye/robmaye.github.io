# Welcome to Silicon Sentience

## What Will You Find Here

In this cognitive enclave, we will journey through the labyrinth of Artificial Intelligence, Cognitive Architectures, and their existential tapestry. We will examine topics ranging from the tantalizing prospect of [Longevity Escape Velocity](#) to the ethical scaffolds like the '2 Assumptions Theory.' Each topic is not just an exploration but a piece of a larger puzzle that defines our collective aspiration for a better future.

## Why I Write

As this inaugural post materializes, I find myself in Donegal—a slice of idyllic wilderness in Ireland's lush tapestry. One might ask: Why trade this serenity to dive into the maelstrom of AI? The answer is simple yet profound: ethical imperative. The burgeoning landscape of AI is not just a technological frontier but a philosophical one. My journey through this space is not led by ego but a collective concern. If those with altruistic and informed perspectives opt for silence, then we bequeath our future to those propelled by mere instrumentality—power, money, control. And that's a dice roll humanity can ill afford.

## A Brief Introduction

I am an AI researcher from the UK, recently honored with a First Class degree in Computer Science from Southampton University. I'm currently navigating the academic corridors of Imperial College London, delving further into this fascinating domain. 

During my undergrad, I stumbled upon the revelatory works of David Shapiro on natural language cognitive architectures. His perspectives on building benevolent systems aligned perfectly with my own: reduce suffering, increase understanding, and advance prosperity. These objectives are not just lofty ideals but a framework for creating agents that reflect humanity's best.

## What Next?

Who knows what form this blog will take? I don't. I simply wish to echo my thoughts into the digital cosmos and keenly observe the reflections. With that said, dear reader, welcome to Silicon Sentience. May this space inspire, educate, and provoke thought.
