# Why 
As I write this first passage in hopefully a long series of articles, I am heading up to donegal, a remote idylic wilderness in the depths of ireland.
Part of me is questioning why I should even bother to spend time writing about AI, when I've such a beautiful life I could settle down into - knowing what I know about the field, I couldn't on good conscience step back and just let things take their course. Some things are bigger than us all, and the potential evolutionary step in the wave of this new AI movement is too big to just pass up and hope that everyone else gets right. Now this isn't a conceited statement, no single man or woman is the driver of such a vast shift in reality alone, but on this principle, I must take action to attempt steer humanity towards the best-case utopia outcome as, if everyone who knew enough and was philanthropic too make good effective and aligned change in the field stepped back, leaving efforts in the hands of those driven by instrumentally convergent goals (power seeking, money etc..) then I don't have much hope for our future.
